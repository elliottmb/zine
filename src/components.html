What is Kerning?

Kerning concerns the spacing between two specific characters. It's essential for optically balanced letterforms and readability.

Why It Matters

The difference between good kerning and poor kerning dramatically affects typography's visual impact.

Modern Design

Professional designers spend considerable time fine-tuning letter spacing to achieve optical balance. This attention to detail elevates design from amateur to polished.

The Future of Magazine Design
By Creative Director • March 21, 2026
Magazines have been a cornerstone of visual communication for centuries. Yet there's something timeless about the printed magazine—the tactile quality, the carefully composed spreads.
Today's zines are reclaiming that spirit of experimental design. They blend retro aesthetics with modern sensibilities, creating publications that feel nostalgic yet contemporary.

Design Principles

The most successful designs follow core principles: clear hierarchy, generous whitespace, purposeful color, and typographic restraint.

Quick Facts
Hierarchy: Guide the reader's eye through intentional sizing.

Color: Use expressively to reinforce mood.

Typography: Limit fonts to 2-3 families.

Whitespace: Give content breathing room.
